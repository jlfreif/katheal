## Story

### Spread 1: mi-01.yaml
**Node Type:** Solo | **Beat:** Ordinary world + desire hint

#### Description

Micah bounces through his day in constant motion, running and climbing, until he discovers a shimmering doorway that appears only when he pauses for just a moment.

#### Page 5 (Left) - Character in their ordinary world

**Visual:**
Micah (9 years old, black hair, athletic half-Japanese build, medium height) vaults over
a park bench mid-run, arms extended for balance. His body is captured in dynamic motion,
one foot touching the bench, the other in the air. Behind him, a trail of scattered
belongings marks his path: a dropped jacket, an abandoned skateboard, a forgotten water
bottle. Other kids play in the background but Micah moves faster than all of them. His
expression shows pure energy and restlessness. The park is full of afternoon sunlight.

**Text:**
Micah couldn't stay still. Ever. He ran when other kids walked. He climbed when they
sat. His body needed to move, move, move, or it felt like he'd explode. In the park,
he vaulted benches and raced invisible opponents. Stopping felt impossible. Stopping
felt wrong. He was always chasing the next thing.

#### Page 6 (Right) - First hint of something different

**Visual:**
Micah has stopped mid-run to tie his shoe, kneeling on one knee. In this rare moment
of stillness, a shimmering portal of golden light appears before him, hovering between
two trees. His face shows surprise as he looks up from his laces, mouth open in an
astonished "whoa." Golden threads drift out from the portal, moving gently in contrast
to his usual frantic energy. One thread floats close to his face. The portal pulses
softly, waiting. He's frozen in this unusual pause.

**Text:**
But then his shoelace came untied and he had to stop. Just for a second. That's when
he saw it: a doorway made of light, shimmering between the trees. It hadn't been there
before. His hand paused on his lace. The light pulsed gently, like it was breathing.
Without thinking, Micah reached toward it. The moment his fingers touched the glow, everything shifted.

### Spread 3: mi-03.yaml
**Node Type:** Solo | **Beat:** Attempt #1 (simple/obvious)

#### Description

Micah tries to untangle the threads by darting around quickly, grabbing and pulling, but his frantic movement only makes the tangles worse and drives the threads away.

#### Page 9 (Left) - Trying the obvious solution

**Visual:**
Micah sprints through a clearing in Kathaél, arms outstretched trying to catch golden
threads that float in the air. His body is a blur of motion, captured mid-leap as he
jumps to reach a high thread. Threads scatter away from him like startled birds, their
light flickering nervously. His expression shows intense focus and determination. The
forest around him seems to pull back from his frantic energy. Leaves swirl in the wake
of his movement. A tangle of threads hovers just out of reach, pulling tighter.

**Text:**
Micah saw the tangled threads and knew exactly what to do: catch them, untangle them,
fix them. Fast. He darted through the clearing, hands grabbing for the glowing strands.
But they slipped away before he could catch them. He ran faster, jumped higher. The
threads scattered like frightened fish, tangling worse with each frantic grab.

#### Page 10 (Right) - Realizing it doesn't work

**Visual:**
Micah stands panting in the center of the clearing, hands on his knees, catching his
breath. Around him, threads hang in worse tangles than before, their light dimmer and
more agitated. Some have knotted together into tight clumps. His face shows frustration
and confusion, sweat on his brow. A wise old owl perches on a nearby branch, watching
him with knowing eyes. The forest is perfectly still except for Micah's heavy breathing.
A single calm thread hovers far away, as if keeping its distance.

**Text:**
Micah stopped, breathing hard. The threads were worse now. Tighter. Darker. He'd made
things worse by trying to help. An owl blinked at him from a branch. "Speed isn't always
strength," it said quietly. Micah wanted to argue, to run again, to do something. But
the owl just watched, patient and still. Maybe fast wasn't the answer. But what else did he know how to do?

### Spread 6: mi-06.yaml
**Node Type:** Solo | **Beat:** Midpoint: new insight / pattern

#### Description

Micah collapses from exhaustion in the chaotic grove and, in forced stillness, finally notices that the threads calm when he stops moving.

#### Page 15 (Left) - Recognizing what doesn't work

**Visual:**
Micah has collapsed onto the ground, sprawled on his back, completely exhausted. His
chest heaves as he gasps for air. Around him, the whirlwind continues but he's too
tired to move anymore. Sweat drips down his face. His limbs feel like lead. The
hummingbird has zipped away to bother someone else. For the first time in the story,
Micah is completely, utterly still, not by choice but by necessity. His eyes are closed.
The chaotic threads continue their frantic dance above him.

**Text:**
Micah couldn't move anymore. His legs gave out and he collapsed, breathing so hard his
lungs burned. The hummingbird buzzed off to find faster prey. He lay there, forced into
stillness for the first time in his life. Every muscle screamed. His heart hammered.
Moving faster hadn't helped. It had only made him too tired to help at all.

#### Page 16 (Right) - Discovering a new pattern

**Visual:**
Micah lies still on the ground, eyes now open and watching. Above him, the chaotic
threads are beginning to slow their frantic movement. As his breathing calms, the
threads calm too. One thread drifts down toward his outstretched hand, moving gently
instead of wildly. His expression shows dawning wonder and realization. The whirlwind
around the clearing starts to ease. Golden light filters through more peacefully. The
correlation is clear: his stillness creates their stillness.

**Text:**
As he lay there catching his breath, something changed. The wild threads above him
started to slow. His breathing slowed. The threads slowed with it. One floated down
to hover above his hand, calm and gentle. Micah's eyes went wide. They weren't matching
his speed anymore. They were matching his stillness. The pattern clicked. Stop moving.
Start breathing. That was the answer.

The threads he'd calmed begin to drift back toward chaos as his energy spikes again.

**Text:**
But then his body betrayed him. He jumped up, unable to sit another second. "I can't
do this!" The words burst out. Near the Heart Crystal, the biggest tangles still
pulsed dark and tight. Those needed someone who could be still for longer than a few
breaths. Someone who could stay calm when everything felt urgent. He looked at his
shaking hands. Was he even capable of that kind of stillness?
