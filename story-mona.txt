## Story

### Spread 1: mo-01.yaml
**Node Type:** Solo | **Beat:** Ordinary world + desire hint

#### Description

Little Mona discovers a shimmering doorway in her New York City apartment that leads to a magical realm filled with golden threads and light.

#### Page 5 (Left) - Character in their ordinary world

**Visual:**
Mona (2 years old, dark curly hair, bright curious eyes, medium height for her age)
sits on a colorful play mat in a sunny New York City apartment. Wooden blocks scatter
around her as she builds a wobbly tower. Sunlight streams through a large window showing
city buildings outside. Toys dot the floor around her: stuffed animals, picture books, a
small xylophone. She tilts her head, looking at something just beyond the window where
the light seems to shimmer strangely. Her expression shows pure curiosity.

**Text:**
Mona stacked another block on her tower, giggling when it wobbled but stayed up. She
loved making things. Her apartment was full of sunshine and toys and places to explore.
But something caught her eye near the window. The light didn't look quite right, like
it was dancing when nothing moved.

#### Page 6 (Right) - First hint of something different

**Visual:**
Mona stands close to the window where a shimmering portal of golden light has appeared,
hovering in the air like a vertical pool of sunshine. Her small hand reaches toward it,
fingers spread wide with wonder. Golden threads drift out from the portal, floating past
her face like gentle fireflies. The normal city view through the window contrasts sharply
with the magical shimmer. Her eyes are wide with delight, mouth open in an excited "oh!"
The portal pulses softly, inviting and warm.

**Text:**
Mona toddled closer. The strange light grew brighter, forming a doorway made of pure
sunshine. Golden threads floated through it like birthday ribbons. She'd never seen
anything so pretty. Her hand stretched out, and the moment her fingers touched the
light, everything changed.

### Spread 2: mo-02.yaml
**Node Type:** Solo | **Beat:** Inciting incident

#### Description

Mona steps into Kathaél and discovers beautiful golden threads that are tangled and dim, deciding with her innocent courage to help them.

#### Page 7 (Left) - Discovery of the problem

**Visual:**
Mona stands in a luminous forest clearing in Kathaél, surrounded by ancient trees with
glowing moss at their bases. Golden threads fill the air around her, but many are knotted
and tangled together, their light flickering weakly. Some threads droop toward the ground,
lifeless. Mona's expression shifts from wonder to concern as she notices a particularly
sad-looking cluster of threads hanging limp near her face. She reaches up to touch one
gently. The clearing has a twilight quality, beautiful but troubled.

**Text:**
Mona gasped. She stood in a forest that glowed. Golden threads floated everywhere, but
something was wrong. Many were twisted into knots, their light going dim. A thread
drifted past her cheek, barely glowing at all. It looked sad. Mona's brow furrowed.
Why were the pretty threads hurting?

#### Page 8 (Right) - Decision to act

**Visual:**
Mona crouches down, both hands reaching toward a tangle of threads resting on the mossy
ground. Her face shows pure determination despite her young age, tilted head focused on
the problem. A small luminous rabbit sits nearby, watching her with interest. The threads
near Mona begin to glow slightly brighter, responding to her fearless presence. Her curly
hair catches the golden light. Behind her, paths branch out in multiple directions through
the forest, all lined with more threads waiting to be helped.

**Text:**
Mona didn't know words like "magic" or "problem" yet. But she knew when something
needed help. At home, when her blocks fell down, she built them back up. When her
stuffed bunny looked lonely, she gave it hugs. These threads needed help too. She
reached for the nearest tangle. "I fix!"

### Spread 3: mo-03.yaml
**Node Type:** Solo | **Beat:** Attempt #1 (simple/obvious)

#### Description

Mona tries to untangle the threads like she would her toys at home, discovering they respond differently than expected, but her fearless approach begins to help.

#### Page 9 (Left) - Trying the obvious solution

**Visual:**
Mona sits cross-legged on the mossy ground, surrounded by golden threads. She pulls at
a knotted cluster with both hands, tongue poking out in concentration like she does when
playing with her toys. The threads shimmer and shift but don't untangle the way she
expects. Some float away from her grasp like bubbles. Her expression shows puzzlement but
no frustration. Luminous mushrooms glow softly around her. A small blue bird perches on a
nearby branch, tilting its head to watch her work.

**Text:**
Mona grabbed the tangled threads and pulled, just like when her necklaces got knotted.
But these threads slipped through her fingers like water. She tried again, more gently.
They floated away before she could catch them. "Silly threads," she said, not mad, just
curious. They didn't work like toys at all.

#### Page 10 (Right) - Realizing it doesn't work but discovering something

**Visual:**
Mona stops pulling and simply sits still, one hand resting palm-up on her knee. A single
golden thread hovers above her open palm, glowing brighter than before. Her face shows
pure wonder and delight as the thread begins to slowly unwind itself, responding to her
calm presence. More threads drift closer to her, drawn by her fearless, patient energy.
The blue bird has flown down to sit near her foot. Sunlight filters through leaves above,
creating dappled patterns on the moss.

**Text:**
Mona stopped trying to fix them and just sat very still. A thread floated to her hand
all by itself. It glowed brighter, warmer. She giggled softly, and the thread began to
unknot on its own, like it wanted to be untangled. "Oh!" More threads drifted closer.
Maybe they needed something different than pulling. Maybe they just needed a friend.

### Spread 4: ma-mo-04.yaml (joint with MA)
**Node Type:** Meeting Node | **Beat:** Attempt #2 (ask the expert)

#### Description

Maya and little Mona find each other at a studio where colors refuse to stay on canvas, discovering that creative courage might hold an unexpected answer.

#### Page 11 (Left) - Seeking guidance from creativity

**Visual:**
A magical artist's studio built in the branches of an ancient tree, sunlight
streaming through large windows. Canvases line the walls, but their paintings
are fading like watercolors left in rain. Maya (9 years old, sandy-blond hair
with pink streak, bold and athletic) stands at an easel, brush in hand, watching
in frustration as the blue she just applied drains to gray. Paint supplies scatter
the wooden floor. Through the window, golden threads drift past, their light
flickering weakly.

**Text:**
Maya dragged her brush across the canvas. Brilliant blue bloomed for just a second,
then faded to nothing. "Come on," she muttered, trying again. Same thing. Every
color she tried turned gray within heartbeats. The studio that usually burst with
life felt like it was holding its breath, waiting for something.

#### Page 12 (Right) - Receiving direction through fearless creativity

**Visual:**
Little Mona (2 years old, dark curly hair, bright curious eyes) sits on a stool
beside Maya, painting with fearless abandon. Where Mona's brush touches the canvas,
colors STAY - vivid reds, brilliant yellows, deep purples. Maya leans in, amazed,
watching this tiny artist create with complete confidence. Mona grins, tilting her
head as she adds another bold stroke. The golden threads outside pulse brighter,
responding to Mona's courage. Maya's canvas begins to hold color where she paints
without fear.

**Text:**
A small voice piped up. "Paint with me!" Mona stood at the next easel, her brush
already moving. Where she painted, colors blazed and held. Maya stared. "How are
you doing that?" Mona giggled, adding a sunburst of yellow. "Just be you!" Maya
took a breath, pushed away her worries, and painted. This time, the color stayed.

**Meeting Location:** An artist's studio in the trees where paintings lose their color as fast as they're applied

**Shared Action:** Both children paint side by side, watching their work fade, until Mona's fearless strokes reveal that courage creates lasting color
