### Spread 1: mo-01.yaml
**Node Type:** Solo | **Beat:** Ordinary world + desire hint

#### Description

Mona plays with blocks in her room when they begin to glow and stack themselves magically.

#### Page 5 (Left) - Character in their ordinary world

**Visual:**
Mona's playroom in her New York City apartment, colorful foam mats on the floor, toys
scattered in cheerful chaos. Mona (2 years old, wavy short dark brown ringlets, wearing
light blue overalls with brooklyn shirt and white sneakers) sits surrounded by wooden blocks,
stacking them with intense toddler concentration. Her chunky hands place blocks carefully,
tongue between teeth in focus. Some blocks already stacked, others waiting their turn. A
stuffed bunny watches nearby. Sunlight streams through the window. Her expression shows
pure joy in simple play. The scene captures toddler life: exploring, experimenting,
delighting in small accomplishments.

**Text:**
Mona stacked blocks, one on top of another, tongue sticking out as she concentrated. Red
block. Then blue. Then yellow. She clapped when they didn't fall. "Tower!" she announced
proudly to her bunny. Building things was fun. Each block was a new try, a new challenge.
Sometimes they fell. That was okay. She just tried again.

#### Page 6 (Right) - First hint of something different

**Visual:**
The blocks begin to glow with soft golden light. Mona pulls her hands back, eyes going
wide with amazement and a touch of uncertainty. The blocks start stacking themselves,
floating gently into the air, building a tower taller than any she could reach. Golden
threads connect the blocks, making them dance and spin. One block hovers in front of her
face, glowing warm and friendly, as if inviting her to play a new game. Her expression
shifts from surprise to delighted curiosity—that toddler moment of "what's THIS?!" The
atmosphere sparkles with gentle magic designed not to frighten but to invite.

**Text:**
The blocks glowed gold and floated up all by themselves. Mona's eyes got big. "Whoa!" The
blocks stacked higher and higher, spinning and dancing in the air. One floated close to her
face, warm and friendly. She reached out with one finger to touch it. The block giggled—
actually giggled—and bobbed like it wanted to play. New things were sometimes scary. But
also exciting. "Play?" she asked the glowing block.
