## Story

### Spread 1: mx-01.yaml
**Node Type:** Solo | **Beat:** Ordinary world + desire hint

#### Description

Max works on an invention in his room when something magical disrupts his careful calculations.

#### Page 5 (Left) - Character in their ordinary world

**Visual:**
Max's room transformed into an inventor's workshop. A desk covered with circuit boards,
wires, batteries, and half-built gadgets. Max (12 years old, straight red hair parted down
the middle, tall and slim, wearing black t-shirt and light blue basketball shorts) hunches
over a small mechanical device, soldering iron in hand, tongue between teeth in concentration.
His trombone case leans in the corner. Blueprints and calculations cover the walls. A
basketball sits on his bed. His latest experiment—a device meant to sort things automatically—
sits partially assembled. His expression shows intense focus mixed with analytical problem-
solving. The room feels organized chaos, every tool precisely placed.

**Text:**
Max adjusted the tiny gear one more time, checking his calculations. The automatic sorter
should work—the math was perfect, the design logical. He flipped the switch. Gears whirred,
then clunked to a stop. Failed again. He grabbed his notebook, scribbling notes about what
went wrong. Every failure taught him something. He'd figure this out eventually. Science
demanded patience.

#### Page 6 (Right) - First hint of something different

**Visual:**
The failed invention suddenly sparks with golden light, gears spinning on their own, sorting
not objects but beams of light into different colors. Max jerks back in his chair, eyes
wide, watching as golden threads pour from the device and weave through his room like
intelligent light. The threads sort themselves into patterns: questions here, answers there,
possibilities floating between. One thread forms words in the air: "Some problems need
exploration, not calculation." His blueprints flutter despite no wind. The basketball
levitates slightly. His expression shifts from shock to fascinated curiosity. The atmosphere
crackles with impossible possibility.

**Text:**
The device blazed with golden light, working but not the way he'd designed. Threads poured
out, sorting themselves into patterns his math hadn't predicted. Beautiful, impossible
patterns. Words formed in the light: "Some problems need exploration, not calculation."
Max's analytical brain wanted explanations, but the threads didn't wait for logic. One curled
around his wrist, tugging playfully toward somewhere beyond equations and blueprints.

### Spread 2: mx-02.yaml
**Node Type:** Solo | **Beat:** Inciting incident

#### Description

Max discovers Kathaél's broken mechanisms and realizes they failed because someone tried to over-engineer them.

#### Page 7 (Left) - Discovery of the problem

**Visual:**
A vast clearing filled with broken mechanical wonders—clockwork creatures frozen mid-step,
crystal bridges that should connect but don't, water wheels stopped above flowing streams.
Max stands in the center, turning in amazement and growing alarm. Everything is beautifully
designed but completely non-functional. Gears scattered across moss. Broken springs. A
magnificent clockwork bird lies on its side, wings half-extended. Golden threads that
should power these mechanisms hang limp and grey. Max crouches to examine a gear, his
expression showing wonder mixed with the detective-mind clicking into action.

**Text:**
Max stepped into a graveyard of failed inventions. Clockwork wonders lay broken everywhere—
each one brilliantly designed, perfectly calculated, completely overthought. He picked up
a gear. The craftsmanship was flawless, but something about it felt forced, joyless. These
mechanisms hadn't failed because of bad design. They'd failed because whoever made them
had forgotten to let magic be playful.

#### Page 8 (Right) - Decision to act

**Visual:**
Max stands before a particular clockwork creature—a deer made of brass and crystal, frozen
mid-leap. Its internal mechanisms visible through crystal panels show perfect engineering
but no life. Max places both hands on it gently, his expression shifting from analytical
to empathetic. A faint golden thread connects from his hands to the deer's heart-mechanism.
Around him, other broken machines seem to lean in hopefully. His face shows determination—
not to fix them with formulas, but to understand what they need. The atmosphere shifts from
observation to commitment.

**Text:**
One clockwork deer caught his eye, frozen mid-leap, beautiful and dead. Max touched its
crystal heart, feeling the mechanism inside—perfect gears, precise timing, zero joy. He
understood suddenly. These inventions needed more than engineering. They needed someone to
play with them, experiment, try things just to see what happened. "I can help you," he
promised the deer. Not with calculations. With curiosity.

### Spread 3: mx-03.yaml
**Node Type:** Solo | **Beat:** Attempt #1 (simple/obvious)

**Text:**

**Visual:**

dims. Around him, other mechanisms seem to retreat, losing hope. His perfect calculations

brass eyes whirring as it focuses on him. The atmosphere feels like deflating hope.

**Text:**
The deer moved three stiff steps and froze solid, worse than before. Max caught it as it
fell, staring in disbelief. His math was perfect. His execution flawless. But the deer felt
more dead than ever, locked up by too much precision. A mechanical owl clicked and whirred
above him. "Perfect calculations make perfect mechanisms," it said. "But Kathaél needs
magic, not math. You must learn to play."
