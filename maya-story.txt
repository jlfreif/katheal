### Spread 1: ma-01.yaml
**Node Type:** Solo | **Beat:** Ordinary world + desire hint

#### Description

Maya paints in her room when colors begin acting magical and alive.

#### Page 5 (Left) - Character in their ordinary world

**Visual:**
Maya's room in Madrid, walls covered with her bold, colorful artwork. Maya (9 years old,
wavy shoulder-length sandy-blond hair with distinctive pink streak, athletic build, wearing
light blue jeans and pink sweater) stands before an easel, paintbrush in hand, working on
a vibrant abstract painting. Paint splatters dot the floor in rainbow colors. Her art shows
bold strokes and confident color choices—nothing timid or careful. Dance shoes hang on
the wall beside Spanish vocabulary flashcards. Her expression shows fierce concentration
and joy. The room reflects her personality: spirited, bold, authentic.

**Text:**
Maya slashed pink across the canvas, then added electric blue without hesitation. Her art
teacher always said to plan compositions carefully, but Maya painted from her gut—bold,
wild, completely herself. She grabbed orange next, swirling it through the pink. The colors
sang together, bright and unafraid. This was how she thought, how she felt, how she showed
the world who she really was.

#### Page 6 (Right) - First hint of something different

**Visual:**
The paint on Maya's canvas begins to glow and move, colors swirling on their own, lifting
off the canvas into three-dimensional streams of light. Maya steps back, paintbrush frozen
mid-air, eyes wide with amazement. The pink she just painted flows into the air like ribbon,
the blue follows, the orange dances around them both. They weave together creating patterns
more beautiful than she'd planned, forming words in the air: "Your colors need a bigger
canvas." Her room's walls seem to shimmer. The atmosphere crackles with colorful magic and
unlimited possibility.

**Text:**
The paint lifted off the canvas, flowing into the air like liquid light. Maya gasped as
her colors danced together, weaving patterns she'd never imagined. Pink and blue and orange
spiraled into words: "Your colors need a bigger canvas." The walls of her room shimmered,
barely containing the magic. One ribbon of pink curled around her wrist, tugging playfully
toward somewhere that could hold all her bold, authentic colors.

### Spread 2: ma-02.yaml
**Node Type:** Solo | **Beat:** Inciting incident

#### Description

Maya discovers a world where colors are fading and realizes people stopped being themselves.

#### Page 7 (Left) - Discovery of the problem

**Visual:**
A vast meadow in Kathaél where everything should be colorful but isn't—flowers faded to
grey, grass muted to dull green, sky pale and lifeless. Maya stands in the center, turning
in dismay. Ribbons of color that should flow through the air hang limp and transparent.
Creatures that should be vibrant sit grey and still. A rainbow that arcs across the sky
is only three faint colors instead of seven. Maya crouches to touch a grey flower, her
expression showing concern and determination. The atmosphere feels muted and sad, like the
world forgot how to be bold.

**Text:**
Maya stepped into a world drained of color. Everywhere she looked, grey and faded and dim.
Flowers that should blaze with life sat colorless and drooping. The sky hung pale and tired.
She touched a grey petal. It felt wrong, like someone had told it to be quieter, smaller,
less itself. This world had forgotten how to be bold. How to be authentic. How to shine.

#### Page 8 (Right) - Decision to act

**Visual:**
Maya stands before a particular section of the faded meadow, pulling out a small travel
paint set from her pocket. She dips her finger in pink paint and touches a grey flower.
For a moment, pink blooms where she touched, bright and alive. Then it fades back to grey.
But the attempt matters—a golden thread appears faintly, responding to her authentic action.
Her face shows determination and defiance. Around her, the faded world seems to lean in
hopefully. The atmosphere shifts from observation to action, from sadness to brave purpose.

**Text:**
Maya pulled out the small paint set she always carried. She touched pink to a grey flower,
watching color bloom and then fade. But for that second, it had been vibrant and real. A
golden thread appeared, faint but present. Kathaél was responding to her boldness. "I can
help you remember," she told the faded meadow. "I'll show you how to be yourself again."
Even if she didn't know how yet.

**Visual:**

stepped back, heart pounding. Being yourself might be scary. But disappearing was worse.
