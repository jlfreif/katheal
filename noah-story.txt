## Story

### Spread 1: no-01.yaml
**Node Type:** Solo | **Beat:** Ordinary world + desire hint

#### Description

Noah works on his latest story, struggling to find the perfect ending, when something magical interrupts.

#### Page 5 (Left) - Character in their ordinary world

**Visual:**
Noah's desk in his room, covered with notebooks, loose papers, and sticky notes. Noah (12
years old, sandy blond voluminous hair, medium build, wearing blue hoodie and tan sport
pants) hunches over his laptop, fingers flying across the keyboard. Multiple browser tabs
show research for his story. Crumpled papers surround his wastebasket showing crossed-out
attempts. His ukulele leans against the desk. Bookshelves line the walls, stuffed with
adventure novels. His expression shows intense concentration mixed with frustration. The
room is cluttered but organized in his own system. Late afternoon light streams through
the window.

**Text:**
Noah stared at the blinking cursor, willing the right words to appear. He'd rewritten
this ending seventeen times, and none of them felt right. Each version sat crumpled in
the wastebasket, proof that knowing a story needed an ending didn't mean knowing what
that ending should be. He backspaced the whole paragraph again. There had to be a perfect
solution. He just had to find it.

#### Page 6 (Right) - First hint of something different

**Visual:**
Noah's laptop screen glitches, pixels rearranging into golden threads that seem to flow
out of the screen into the real world. Noah leans back in his chair, eyes wide, watching
as the golden threads weave through the air between him and the laptop, forming words
that shimmer: "Some stories need living, not writing." The threads pulse with warm light,
beckoning. His notebooks flutter their pages despite no breeze. One thread reaches toward
his hand as if inviting him to grab it. His expression shifts from frustration to amazed
curiosity. The atmosphere crackles with unexpected possibility.

**Text:**
The screen flickered. Golden threads poured from his laptop into the real world, weaving
through the air like liquid light. Words formed in the threads: "Some stories need living,
not writing." Noah's analytical mind tried to make sense of it, but the threads didn't
wait for logic. One curled around his wrist, warm and insistent, pulling gently toward
somewhere his ordinary room couldn't reach. Adventure, his favorite genre, was calling him
to participate.
